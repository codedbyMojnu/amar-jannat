# A. Parts of Speech — সহজ বাংলায় পূর্ণাঙ্গ গাইড

ছোট গল্প: রাফি পরীক্ষার হলে গেল। প্রশ্নে লেখা— “Each of the students … present.” রাফি থামল— was নাকি were? ঠিক উত্তর: was. কেন? নিচে ধাপে ধাপে শিখি, যেন এমন প্রশ্ন চোখে পড়লেই আপনি “দেখলেই মার্ক” তুলে নিতে পারেন।

## 1) Noun — Determiner, Gender, Number ⭐

সংজ্ঞা: Noun মানে ব্যক্তি/বস্তু/স্থান/ধারণার নাম। BCS-এ বেশি আসে Determiner/Article, Number (Singular/Plural), Gender (Occasional)।

### Determiners (a/an, the, this/that, each/every, some/any, much/many, few/a few, little/a little) ⭐

- a/an: singular countable noun-এর আগে। an + vowel sound: an hour, an honest man.
- the: নির্দিষ্ট/বিশেষ কিছু, unique (the sun), superlative (the best), নদী/সমুদ্র/পাহাড়শ্রেণি (the Jamuna, the Himalayas)।
- zero article: language, meals, games-এর আগে সাধারণত article নয় (at breakfast, play cricket, learn English)।
- each/every: singular verb নেয়। Every student is…; Each of + plural noun হলেও verb singular: Each of the students was…
- much/many; few/a few; little/a little: uncountable vs countable পার্থক্য খেয়াল করুন।

🧠 Common Mistake: “He is in the hospital” vs “He is in hospital.” British usage-এ in hospital (as a patient) ঠিক; তবে BCS-এ সাধারণ নিয়ম শিখুন, context দেখুন।

### Number (Singular/Plural) ⭐

- Irregular plural: child→children, man→men, datum→data, medium→media, criterion→criteria.
- Uncountable nouns singular verb নেয়: news, advice, information, furniture, luggage.
- Collective nouns সাধারণত singular: The team is…; তবে sense অনুযায়ী plural হতে পারে (The team are fighting among themselves)। BCS-এ সাধারণত singular দিন।

### Gender (Overview)

- Common pairs: actor/actress (modern usage-এ actor), prince/princess; nephew/niece.

BCS MCQ (Typical):
Which article completes the sentence? “He is … honest man.”

- a
- an ✅
- the
- no article
  ব্যাখ্যা: honest → vowel sound → an.

## 2) Pronoun — Agreement & Case ⭐

মূল লক্ষ্য: Pronoun-antecedent agreement, case (subjective/objective), distributive/indefinite pronoun usage।

- Each/either/neither + singular verb: Either of the answers is correct.
- Everyone/Everybody সাধারণত singular sense—verb singular; formal writing-এ “his/her”, modern usage-এ “their” দেখা যায়। BCS-এ spesso singular sense ধরা হয়।
- Who/whom: who = subject, whom = object; “Whom do you want to meet?”
- Relative pronoun: that/who/which নির্বাচন—persons (who), things (which), restrictive (that) — প্রেক্ষিত দেখুন।

🧠 Common Mistake: “One should do his duty.” → inclusive হলে “One should do one’s duty.”

BCS MCQ (Typical):
Fill in: “Each of the boys … given a prize.”

- were
- are
- is ✅
- have

## 3) Verb — Finite/Non-finite, Linking, Phrasal, Modals ⭐⭐

এখানেই সর্বাধিক নম্বর ঝরে। Subject-Verb Agreement (SVA) + Verb forms + Modals + Phrasal verbs—প্রশ্ন প্রায়ই আসে।

### Finite vs Non-finite

- Finite verb subject/tense অনুযায়ী বদলায়: He writes/They write.
- Non-finite: infinitive (to go), gerund (going), participle (gone/going). “I like to read.” “Reading books is fun.”

### Linking Verb

- be/feel/seem/become/look/sound/taste ইত্যাদি। Complement নেয়, adverb নয়: “She looks happy.” (adjective), not “happily”.

### Phrasal Verbs ⭐

- look after (take care of), give up (stop doing), put off (postpone), bring about (cause), look into (investigate).
  BCS-এ অর্থ নির্ণয় ভিত্তিক MCQ জনপ্রিয়।

### Modals ⭐

- can/could, may/might, must/should, have to/need, ought to—pattern: modal + base verb.
- Probability/obligation/permission nuance খেয়াল করুন। “You must obey the rules.” “He might come.”

🧠 Common Mistake: “He is used to swim.” → “He is used to swimming.” / “He used to swim.” (দুটোর মানে আলাদা)।

BCS MCQ (Typical):
Choose the correct option: “He … finish the work today.”

- should ✅
- have to
- ought to
- might
  ব্যাখ্যা: obligation/suggestion context—multiple can be valid, কিন্তু সাধারণ duty/suggestion-এ should প্রাধান্য পায়।

## 4) Adjective — Degree, Order, Adjective vs Adverb ⭐

- Adjective order: opinion, size, age, shape, color, origin, material, purpose + noun (a nice small old round red Italian wooden coffee table)।
- Adjective vs Adverb: “He runs fast” (adverb), “He is fast” (adjective)। “She feels bad” (adjective), not badly.
- Comparative/Superlative: bigger/biggest; more beautiful/most beautiful; irregular: good→better→best.

⚡ Trick: double comparative/superlative নয় (more better ✗, most easiest ✗)।

---

## 5) Adverb — Position & Negative Inversion

- Adverb position অর্থ বদলায়: “Only I spoke” vs “I spoke only” (object-limited)।
- Negative adverb + inversion: Never/Hardly/Scarcely/Rarely had I seen…
- Too…to vs So…that: “too + adj + to” (negative sense), “so + adj + that + can/could.”

---

## 6) Preposition — Time/Place & Fixed Uses ⭐⭐

- Time: at 5 pm, on Monday, in July/1990; Period: for two years; Starting point: since 2010.
- Place: at (point), in (enclosed area/city), on (surface)।
- By vs With: “killed by a tiger” (agent), “cut with a knife” (instrument)।
- Between (two) vs Among (many)।
- Different from/to/than — exam style variation; BCS-এ ‘from’ নিরাপদ।

BCS MCQ (Typical):
Fill in: “He has been living here … 2015.”

- for
- since ✅
- from
- by

---

## 7) Conjunction — Pairing Rules & Agreement ⭐

- Not only … but also — verb agrees with the nearest subject: Not only the teacher but also the students were present.
- Either … or / Neither … nor — verb nearest subject অনুযায়ী: Either the teachers or the headmaster is…
- Though/Although … yet — সাধারণত although ব্যবহারে yet বসে না: Although he is poor, he is honest.
- No sooner … than / Scarcely … when — fixed pairing।

## Previous Pattern Insight & Smart Prediction (BCS) 🧭

যা বেশি আসে:

- Articles/Determiners (a/an/the, each/every, few/little) ⭐⭐
- Pronoun agreement (each/either/neither, who/whom) ⭐
- Subject–Verb Agreement with conjunctions (either…or) ⭐⭐
- Prepositions (since/for, in/at/on, by/with, between/among) ⭐⭐
- Phrasal verbs & Modals অর্থভেদ ⭐
- Adjective vs Adverb confusion, degree formation ⭐

আগামী BCS সাজেশন (নিরাপদ টপিক):

1. a/an/the এর বিশেষ রুল + zero article
2. Each/Either/Neither + singular verb, “one … one’s” usage
3. Phrasal verbs: put off, give up, bring about, look into
4. since vs for; at/in/on; by vs with
5. Either…or / Neither…nor — nearest subject অনুযায়ী verb
6. Linking verb + adjective (look/feel/seem) — adverb নয়

## Practice MCQ (BCS Style)

1. “Each of the players … awarded.”

   - were
   - are
   - is ✅
   - have

2. Choose the correct article: “He is … European.”

   - a ✅
   - an
   - the
   - no article
     ব্যাখ্যা: European → consonant sound /juː/ → a.

3. Pick the correct preposition: “He has been ill … Monday.”

   - for
   - since ✅
   - from
   - at

4. Identify the correct form: “She looks … today.”

   - happily
   - happy ✅
   - happiness
   - more happily

5. Select the right phrasal verb: “Please … smoking.”

   - give in
   - give up ✅
   - give out
   - give away

6. Choose the right pair: “No sooner had he arrived … it started raining.”

   - when
   - than ✅
   - then
   - that

7. Fill in: “Either the teachers or the headmaster … responsible.”

   - are
   - were
   - is ✅
   - have been

8. Correct alternative: “He is used to … early.”
   - get up
   - getting up ✅
   - to get up
   - have got up
